Gerrit Rietveld is a Dutch designer, born June 24, 1888, who became very famous and influential in the field of furniture design. Gerrit believed that early 19th century handmade furniture was too large, heavy, and difficult to produce. He had the idea of creating furniture that could be produced by machines, furniture that would be simpler in style and more affordable for the mass market. After De Stijl magazine published Gerrit Rietveld's work slatted chair in 1919, Gerrit began to become known as a designer and it was the beginning of a meteoric career as a designer.

Style and Cultural Influence

Gerrit Rietveld is closely associated with the De Stijl movement, his work, his style and approach to architecture had an incredible influence on design in Holland and later on design around the world. Gerrit Rietveld's style is based on the geometric interaction of the elements of the design object, the designer often uses perpendicular connections, the parallelism of opposing elements, and contrasting colors to emphasize the geometric form of the object.
